Privacy Policy

Effective date: March 1, 2026  ·  Automaite — Toronto, Canada  ·  Questions? hello@automaite.ca

We are a small, independent product. We collect only what we need to run the service. We do not sell your data, run advertising, or use third-party trackers. This policy explains plainly what we collect, why, and how it is protected.

1. What We Collect

The following table summarizes the personal data we collect and the reason for collecting it:

Data Source Purpose
Google email address Google OAuth sign-in Authentication and account identification
AI API keys You provide them Running terminal sessions on your behalf
Billing information Stripe (payment processor) Processing subscription payments
Terminal session activity Live session data Delivering the terminal service during an active session

We do not collect your name, phone number, physical address, or any demographic information. We do not run analytics software, advertising pixels, or any third-party tracking on our site or application.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect solely to operate and improve the Service:

  • Email address: Used to authenticate your account, associate your subscription, and contact you about critical service matters (e.g., billing failures, policy changes). We do not send marketing emails unless you explicitly opt in.
  • API keys: Passed to the AI CLI agent during your terminal sessions. Keys are never exposed in logs or transmitted to any party other than the designated AI provider endpoint.
  • Billing data: Managed entirely by Stripe. We receive a customer ID and subscription status from Stripe; we never see or store your raw payment card details.
  • Session data: Used only to deliver your active terminal session. Session content is not persisted to disk or reviewed by our team.

3. How We Store and Protect Your Data

We take reasonable technical measures to protect the data you entrust to us:

  • API keys are encrypted at rest using Fernet symmetric encryption (AES-128-CBC with HMAC-SHA256 authentication). Encryption keys are stored separately from the encrypted data.
  • All data in transit between your browser and our servers is protected with TLS.
  • Account data is stored on our servers hosted on DigitalOcean infrastructure in Canada/US. DigitalOcean maintains SOC 2 compliance.
  • Session data is ephemeral and not written to persistent storage beyond the duration of your session.

No security measure is perfect. If we become aware of a breach that affects your personal data, we will notify you at the email address on your account as required by applicable law.

4. What We Share

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal data. We share it only in the narrow circumstances described below.

  • Stripe: We share the minimum information needed to process your subscription payment (email, subscription plan). Stripe's privacy policy governs how they handle that data.
  • AI providers: Your API keys are transmitted to the relevant AI provider (e.g., Anthropic, Google, OpenAI) solely to fulfill your terminal session requests. This is the explicit purpose for which you provide them.
  • Legal obligations: We may disclose your information if required by law, court order, or government request, or if we believe disclosure is necessary to protect the safety or rights of any person.

We have no advertising partners. We do not share data with data brokers, analytics vendors, or any other third parties beyond those listed above.

5. Data Retention

We retain your data only as long as necessary:

  • Account data (email): Retained for the life of your account and deleted within 30 days of account closure.
  • API keys: Retained until you delete them from your account settings, or within 30 days of account closure, whichever comes first.
  • Billing records: Retained as required by financial regulations, typically 7 years, as managed by Stripe.
  • Terminal session data: Not persisted. Session content is discarded when the session ends.

You can delete your API keys at any time from your account settings. To request full account deletion, email us at hello@automaite.ca.

6. Your Rights

Depending on your location, you may have rights regarding your personal data. Regardless of jurisdiction, we commit to the following:

  • Access: You can request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Correction: You can ask us to correct inaccurate data.
  • Deletion: You can request that we delete your account and associated personal data. Email hello@automaite.ca to request deletion.
  • Portability: You can request your data in a machine-readable format.
  • Objection: You can object to certain processing of your data.

We will respond to all requests within 30 days. To exercise any of these rights, contact us at hello@automaite.ca.

If you are a resident of the European Economic Area, you may also have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority.

7. Cookies and Tracking

We use a single session cookie to keep you signed in. This cookie:

  • Is set only after you authenticate with Google.
  • Contains only a session identifier — no personal data is stored in the cookie itself.
  • Expires when you sign out or when the session times out.

We do not use tracking cookies, advertising cookies, or any third-party cookies. We do not use analytics platforms such as Google Analytics, Mixpanel, or similar services. There are no tracking pixels or fingerprinting scripts on our site.

8. Children's Privacy

The Service is not directed at or intended for use by individuals under the age of 18. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children. If you believe a minor has provided us with personal data, please contact us at hello@automaite.ca and we will delete it promptly.

9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we do, we will update the effective date at the top of this page. For material changes, we will attempt to notify you at the email address associated with your account at least 14 days before the changes take effect.

Your continued use of the Service after changes are posted constitutes acceptance of the revised policy. If you do not agree with any changes, you should stop using the Service and request account deletion.
